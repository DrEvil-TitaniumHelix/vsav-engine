Three classic hex wargames, movement rules fully enforced, running entirely in your browser. No install, no server — the engine is ~200 lines of JavaScript ported from the Python original.

How to move

  1. Pick your side in the top bar, then click a counter or stack (stacks give you a picker: one unit, or the whole stack at the slowest MA).
  2. The map dims and the legal destination hexes light up, each labeled with its movement-point cost — terrain, roads, rivers/bridges, walls/hedges, and zones of control are enforced by the engine, not the honor system.
  3. Drag to a lit hex and drop. Illegal drops snap back.
  4. Pass skips a unit; the tracker counts your side's moves. Reset game restores the setup.

Game notes

  • Tobruk: right-click a unit for the facing menu — rotate it onto any of the six hexsides. (Unit movement values are provisional pending the original data cards.)
  • Arnhem: river hexsides are impassable except over bridges — select a unit near the Waal and watch the engine route the long way around. Roads cost ½ MP.
  • ASL: +1 MF to cross walls and hedges (B9.4) — find the gap in the hedge ring at Q4. Squads have 4 MF, leaders 6 (per the official ASLSK rules).

What this is

A demo of a general, spec-driven move-legality engine for VASSAL games. Each game here is pure data — a game.json (grid geometry, sides, movement rules with rulebook cites) plus terrain extracted from the board art itself. The full engine reads and writes real VASSAL 3.x .vsav saves, so VASSAL, this browser UI, and an AI opponent are three interchangeable clients of the same file. This browser demo keeps moves in memory; the save-file layer lives in the source: github.com/DrEvil-TitaniumHelix/vsav-engine (MIT).
